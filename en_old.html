About Old JNR Kurayoshi Line

THE LOST MEMORY OF SHOWA

"The Iron Road that connected the town and mountains, carrying people's lives."
The Old JNR Kurayoshi Line was a railway line of about 20km connecting Agei Station (now Kurayoshi Station) on the San-in Main Line to Yamamori Station in Sekigane Town.
Opened in 1912 (Meiji 45), it served for a long time as a means of transport for local residents and for transporting lumber and other goods.

With the spread of motorization, it sadly ended its history on March 31, 1985 (Showa 60). However, even 40 years after its closure, the rails, platforms, and tunnels retain their appearance from that time.
In particular, the "Abandoned track covered in bamboo groves" around the site of Taikyuji Station continues to fascinate abandoned railway fans and photographers nationwide with its fantastic beauty.
