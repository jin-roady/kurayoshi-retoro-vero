What is the Old Kurayoshi Line?

THE LOST MEMORY OF SHOWA

"Iron roads that connected the city and mountains, carrying people's lives."
The Old Kurayoshi Line was a railway line of about 20km connecting Agei Station (now Kurayoshi Station) on the San-in Main Line to Yamamori Station in Sekigane Town.
Opened in 1912 (Meiji 45), it served for a long time as transportation for residents and key logistics for chrome ore and lumber.

With the spread of motorization, it sadly closed its history on March 31, 1985 (Showa 60). However, even decades after its abandonment, rails, platform ruins, and tunnels remain strongly retaining their appearance from that time.
In particular, the "Abandoned tracks covered in bamboo grove" around the ruins of Taiqyuji Station continue to fascinate railway fans and photographers nationwide with their fantastical beauty.
