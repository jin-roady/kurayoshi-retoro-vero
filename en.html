About Old Kurayoshi Line

THE LOST MEMORY OF SHOWA

"The iron road that connected the city and mountains."
The former JNR Kurayoshi Line was a railway line of about 20km connecting Agei Station (now Kurayoshi Station) on the San-in Main Line to Yamamori Station in Sekigane Town.
Opened in 1912 (Meiji 45), it served as a means of transportation for local residents and for transporting goods such as chrome ore and timber for a long time.

With the spread of motorization, it closed its history on March 31, 1985 (Showa 60). However, even decades after its abolition, the rails, platforms, and tunnels retain their appearance from that time.
In particular, the "Abandoned tracks covered in bamboo forest" around the ruins of Taikyuji Station continue to fascinate railway fans and photographers nationwide with their fantastical beauty.
